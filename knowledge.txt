TOPIC: How to identify your Skin Type
RULES:
- DRY SKIN: Feels tight in morning, rough patches, dull appearance.
- OILY SKIN: Feels slick to touch, shiny appearance, produces oil in T-zone and cheeks, enlarged pores, prone to blackheads/acne.
- COMBINATION SKIN: Dry on cheeks but oily in T-zone (nose/forehead), enlarged pores on nose.
- DEHYDRATED SKIN: Visibly dry/flakey but produces excessive sebum (oil). Often mistaken for oily skin. Treat as dry skin!
- SENSITIVE SKIN: Reacts negatively to products (breakouts/redness), becomes hot when touched, itchy.
- ACNE-PRONE SKIN: Prone to acne, usually combination/oily, products cause flare-ups easily.
- NORMAL SKIN: None of the above, dewy appearance.
